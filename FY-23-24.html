During the financial year 2023–24, Karmanye Foundation continued its core charitable mission of relieving the suffering of community and street animals in and around Pune. During this year the Foundation received its 80G certification (April 2023), enabling donors to claim tax deductions on their contributions. The Foundation operates on a report-driven model: cases are reported by citizens, feeders, guards and volunteers; the animal is picked up and assessed; veterinary treatment or surgery is arranged at private clinics or paid shelters; and, wherever possible, the animal is rehabilitated and released back to its territory or placed in a permanent home. Alongside individual rescues, the Foundation undertook sterilisation and vaccination drives, community feeding and water-bowl provision during summer, senior and palliative care, support for elderly human feeders, and public advocacy for animal welfare and justice.

This report summarises the Foundation's activities month by month across the year. Approximately 39 representative cases have been documented from the Foundation's field records, selected to reflect the range and scale of work undertaken. FY 2023-24 was an earlier, lower-volume year for the Foundation, so several months carry fewer cases. Each monthly entry is a narrative account of the principal cases and initiatives handled during that month.

Section 3

Month-by-Month Activity Record

April 2023 3 cases

Featured: Pillu · Gundu · 80G Certification

April opened a landmark month for the Foundation, which received its 80G certification — a milestone that lets donors claim tax deductions and reflects the organisation's growing formal standing; fittingly, the Foundation takes its name from the Bhagavad Gita's teaching on selfless action. On the ground, the month was dominated by Pillu, a gentle, deeply human-friendly boy admitted with tick-bite paralysis. Over the month he moved from full paralysis to holding his head up and regaining strength in his hind legs, at a recurring cost of about ₹20,000 every seven to eight days — until, late in April, his wounds flared, his leg became infected and he ran a 106°F fever, a frightening setback. Gundu, one of the Foundation's oldest dogs, neared the end of a long recovery from a maggot-infested ear and was almost ready to return home.

May 2023 4 cases

Featured: Nugget · Pillu · Gundu · Arjun

May held the Foundation's best news in a long time — Nugget, after a nine-month wait, was finally adopted into a permanent home. It also held one of its darkest moments: Pillu's treatment costs mounted so steeply that, for the first time, the team agonised over whether it could afford to continue, before resolving to press on as he slowly began gaining weight again. Tragedy struck Gundu, freshly released after her ear treatment, when she was hit by a scooter and left unable to rise; at fourteen she fought hard and stayed remarkably cooperative through her care. Arjun, a community dog reported immobile after a hit-and-run, was picked up, X-rayed and taken in for treatment. It was a month that captured the Foundation's emotional extremes — a joyful adoption alongside wrenching decisions about cost and survival.

June 2023 5 cases

Featured: Lalu · Kalu · Duggya · Patchy · Bhaloo

June brought a cluster of medical cases. Lalu, Bhalu's companion, developed a painful aural haematoma in his ear that resisted on-site treatment; alongside him, Kalu suffered an alarming emergency when blood poured from his nose at a routine check-up, and both were found to have tick fever, with Kalu also nursing a burst elbow abscess. Duggya, a bike-garage owner's dog reported limping in pain, was found to have a hind-leg fracture, casted and placed in private foster. Patchy, a beautiful pup, was diagnosed with rickets brought on by nutritional deficiency from being born on the streets. And Bhaloo, treated and released the previous year, stopped eating and grew listless, prompting the Foundation to step in once more.

July 2023 4 cases

Featured: Sundari · Rocky · Kalu & Lalu · Patchy

July's most harrowing case was Sundari, a runner who took two days to catch and whose critical tumour — dripping blood and infested with maggots — was too advanced for surgery; placed in private foster, she began, remarkably, to improve, the bleeding and maggots gone within a fortnight. Rocky healed beautifully after surgery to remove a small tumour on his head and returned to his territory, his treatment, foster and tests costing around ₹12,700 with a clear biopsy hoped for. Kalu and Lalu returned to their home ground to continue treatment on-site — Kalu on hepatic food and supplements for his liver, Lalu's ear haematoma steadily shrinking. A happy update also came for Patchy.

August 2023 5 cases

Featured: Pillu · Noor · Browny · Shakti · Sundari

August combined surgery, milestones and a fresh emergency. Noor underwent surgery to be spayed and to have a damaged eye removed, recovering well. Pillu reached a moving milestone — after months of paralysis he began walking on wheels and using his front legs, a triumph his caregivers and vets had worked toward all year. Sundari completed a month in foster care, safe, fed and sheltered after her suffering on the roads. A new urgent case arose in Browny, a normally playful dog the Foundation feeds daily, who turned listless and stopped eating and was rushed in for treatment. And Shakti, a pup from a shepherd family camped near a feeding area, was taken up for care.

September 2023 3 cases

Featured: Shakti · Snowy · Yuki

September was a quieter month centred on recovery and rehoming. Shakti recovered and was released back to his shepherd family, who were overjoyed to have him home, with his first vaccinations administered. Snowy was found one afternoon with a swollen, gooey, closed eye, too painful to touch; the Foundation picked her up without delay and took her straight to a canine ophthalmologist. The Foundation also ran an adoption appeal for Yuki, found lost and hassled on the streets, sending out hope that he would find a forever home, alongside a gentle 'share a little of your salary' fundraising drive for community feeding.

October 2023 2 cases

Featured: Browny · Lalu

October's headline was a hard-won recovery: Browny came back to his territory after a fifty-day fight in which he survived distemper and several other complications, finding his way back into his pack — a fighter whose treatment bill reached ₹38,200. The month's difficult case was Lalu, whose investigations revealed a tumour; with Diwali approaching, the Foundation appealed for support to give him, and many like him, a chance to live, openly acknowledging how overwhelming the caseload had become.

November 2023 3 cases

Featured: Bhairoo · Jabya · Lalu

November brought a serious new rescue and a painful loss. Bhairoo, a gentle giant the Foundation adores, was hit by a vehicle and admitted with a pelvic fracture; his surgery, in which a plate was inserted, was completed successfully against an advance of ₹30,000, and he stabilised. Jabya, a free-spirited, trusting boy, was diagnosed with TVT (transmissible venereal tumour) and began treatment. The month closed in grief as Lalu — battling cancer — deteriorated overnight and, on veterinary advice, was gently released from his suffering. The Foundation also marked Diwali with heartfelt thanks to its supporters.

December 2023 2 cases

Featured: Bhairoo · Jabya

December was quieter but focused on two recovering boys. Bhairoo's remarkable story — from a pelvic fracture to walking again after surgery — was shared to rally support, as the Foundation, out of funds, cared for him alongside its other rescues. Mid-month, both Bhairoo and Jabya were visited at the shelter, delighted to see their rescuer; Bhairoo's appetite was still poor and needed watching, but both were on the mend and being reassured that home was not far away.

January 2024 2 cases

Featured: Winter beds · Yuki

January's work continued quietly through the winter. A virtual-adoption appeal was renewed for Yuki, a sterilised, vaccinated Lab-mix still waiting for a home. The Foundation also completed a winter beds project, distributing warm beds to community dogs across Pune to see them through the cold — a small but meaningful comfort for animals living on the streets. Posting slowed as the founder tended to an unwell home dog, but the feeding and care work carried on uninterrupted.

February 2024 1 case

Featured: Jack

February was a month of quiet loss. Jack, a sweet boy the Foundation had been fighting for, lost his battle with life when his small body could take no more, and passed despite every effort made on his behalf. His caregivers marked his passing with love and sorrow — a reminder that, for all the recoveries, some cases end in heartbreak, and that each animal is grieved as one of the Foundation's own.

March 2024 5 cases

Featured: Bhairoo · Julie · Bhola · Sweety · Durga

The year closed with a busy March of rescues, a release and an adoption. Bhairoo, whose long journey began with a pelvic fracture in November, was finally released back to his territory and his companions after surgery and months of recovery — a triumphant close to his story. Julie, a beloved dog outside the founder's society, was treated for infections around her eyes and nose; the medication worked well, though elevated kidney levels meant she needed a little longer before release. Bhola was taken up for a painful ear injury he would not let anyone examine, and Sweety — loving and friendly — was checked for a growing, ulcerating lump on her leg, opening a case that would continue into the following year. An adoption appeal was also shared for the feisty young pup Durga.

This report is a true summary of the charitable activities carried out by Karmanye Foundation during the financial year 1 April 2023 to 31 March 2024, based on the Foundation's field and publication records.
