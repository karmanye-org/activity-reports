The Work

Real rescues. Real people. For more than a decade.

Karmanye Foundation did not begin with paperwork. It began on the street in 2014, with one person feeding and caring for Pune's community dogs — and grew, case by case, into a registered non-profit trusted by an entire neighbourhood.

The method has never changed. A call comes in — from a security guard, a feeder, a tapri owner, a passer-by. The animal is found and assessed; treatment or surgery is arranged at private clinics and paid shelters; recovery is funded by ordinary people chipping in; and wherever possible the animal is healed and returned to its own territory, or placed in a loving home. Around that core run sterilisation and vaccination drives, summer water-bowls and winter beds, senior and palliative care, support for elderly human feeders, and a growing voice for animal welfare and justice. The record below is drawn from more than 835 public updates spanning 2014 to 2026 — the real, day-to-day work of real people.

Threads Across the Years

The same dogs, cared for over time

What sets the Foundation apart is continuity. These are not one-off rescues but the same community animals watched over for years. Zoey, found paralysed after a hit-and-run, recurs from 2020 through 2022 as her seizures are managed. Moti is treated from a leg tumour into a tail amputation across 2021–22. Whitey's 2022 ordeal — paralysed by a botched shelter sterilisation, misdiagnosed, then amputated — ends with her walking home a happy tripod. Browny beats distemper in 2023 and, two years later, survives a multiple-fracture hit-and-run. Through the chemotherapy of Pillya, the tick-paralysis recovery of Pillu, the long surgical road of Bhairoo, and the returning names of Sweety, Baba and Julie, one truth holds: the Foundation stays with an animal for as long as it takes.

Milestones

How the work grew

2014 · The beginning
Documented rescue and feeding work begins on the streets of Pune — the earliest posts date to February 2014.
2017–2020 · Grassroots years
Years of hands-on rescue, adoption drives and community feeding, and a growing circle of supporters — the guards, feeders, fosters, vets and donors who came to be known as the #hoomansofkarmanye.
2021 · Formalised
Incorporated as a Section 8 non-profit company (CIN U85300PN2021NPL203519), giving the work a formal, accountable structure.
2022 · Scale & accountability
Caseload doubles. The Foundation exposes and nurses the survivors of a botched mass-sterilisation at a popular shelter, and fights a parvo outbreak among a rescued litter.
2023 · 80G certification
Receives 80G certification (April 2023), enabling tax-deductible donations — alongside long-haul medical cases and a winter-beds project.
2024–25 · Complexity
Runs multiple high-cost surgeries in parallel, chemotherapy protocols, senior and palliative care, and virtual-adoption programmes.
2025–26 · Advocacy & justice
Extends its mission from rescue to accountability — pressing publicly for justice in a cruelty case and drawing press coverage — at its highest documented caseload yet.
The People

Real work, by real people

Behind every rescue is a network of ordinary people. The Foundation's work runs on the tapri owners who cook for street dogs, the construction-site guards who call at 2 a.m., the fosters who hand-rear orphaned pups, the vets who take midnight emergencies, the drivers who ferry animals across the city, and the hundreds of donors who fund a bill they will never see repaid. They are the #hoomansofkarmanye.
