During the financial year 2022–23, Karmanye Foundation continued its core charitable mission of relieving the suffering of community and street animals in and around Pune. The Foundation operates on a report-driven model: cases are reported by citizens, feeders, guards and volunteers; the animal is picked up and assessed; veterinary treatment or surgery is arranged at private clinics or paid shelters; and, wherever possible, the animal is rehabilitated and released back to its territory or placed in a permanent home. Alongside individual rescues, the Foundation undertook sterilisation and vaccination drives, community feeding and water-bowl provision during summer, senior and palliative care, support for elderly human feeders, and public advocacy for animal welfare and justice.

This report summarises the Foundation's activities month by month across the year. Approximately 48 representative cases have been documented from the Foundation's field records, selected to reflect the range and scale of work undertaken. Each monthly entry is a narrative account of the principal cases and initiatives handled during that month.

Section 3

Month-by-Month Activity Record

April 2022 4 cases

Featured: Tiger · Zoey · Moti · Water bowls

April began under a brutal heat wave. Tiger, only recently recovered from a viral infection, was struck down by heatstroke — listless and off his food for two days — before healing and returning home to his caretaker. In response the Foundation ran a water-bowl drive, distributing bowls with partners so community animals could survive the killing heat. Zoey, who suffers severe, prolonged seizures, relapsed with fits that would not stop and had to be rushed to the vet and admitted to a facility to stabilise, as her hind legs give way during these episodes. Moti continued healing well from an earlier wound, now free of infection, watched over anxiously so he would not disturb it at night.

May 2022 4 cases

Featured: Zoey · Moti · Milo · Jabhya

May brought recoveries and homecomings. Zoey returned about a month after her seizure crisis, walking better than before. Moti went home to his petrol bunk and the people who love him, fully healed — a homecoming that fell fittingly on Akshaya Tritiya. Milo, off-colour and not eating for two days, was rushed in without delay and admitted; diagnosed with a viral infection, congestion and dehydration, he was discharged on a ten-day antibiotic course. No sooner had Milo finished his medicines than his farmhouse companion Jabhya fell ill with similar symptoms — weak, dehydrated and off his food — and was taken up for care in turn.

June 2022 5 cases

Featured: Kevin · Bandu · Jabya · Sterilisation drive

June was a heavy month. Kevin, a tiny abandoned pup found near a feeding area with a torn lower jaw, was rescued and, luckily spared a jaw break, had the bad tear stitched and began a long course of antibiotics and recovery. Bandu, a darling girl, was caught in a hit-and-run, disappeared overnight and returned badly hurt the next morning — the start of a grave case. Jabya remained unwell and, when antibiotics did not help, was sent for deeper investigation. The Foundation also carried out a sterilisation drive, stressing that spaying and neutering — Animal Birth Control — is the humane way to stop litter after litter on the streets. With cases mounting, the founder made a candid birthday-month appeal for support.

July 2022 4 cases

Featured: Kevin · Bandu · Yogi · Gunda

July's cases tested the Foundation's resolve. Kevin remained critical for days — on a drip, not eating and passing blood — before finally, movingly, starting to eat, his stitches healed and his energy returning. Bandu reached a full month in care for a spine fracture that had damaged the nerves controlling her movement and bladder, with no good news yet — one of the year's most heartbreaking cases. Yogi was reported with an extraordinary tumour on his head, a case unlike any the founder had personally encountered. And Gunda, reported with a wound near his hindquarters, was admitted rather than released, since the monsoon made maggot infestation a real danger.

August 2022 5 cases

Featured: Hope & pups · Yogi · Chulbul · Jabya · ABC drive

August held both cruelty and hope. Hope, a mother dog, was found abandoned with her nine newborn puppies after a heartless act witnessed by kind people from a nearby slum — the beginning of a long, fraught chapter. Yogi was treated for tick fever over twenty-one days, after which surgery for his head tumour could be planned once he was cleared. Chulbul healed from her wounds and returned to her territory and friends, still on medication for a fungal infection. Jabya came back with a nasty ear infection and growths on his lip and mouth. The Foundation also sent five community dogs — Ginger, Maggie, Snowie, Milo and Jabya — for sterilisation under the Animal Birth Control programme, a decision that would, tragically, go badly wrong.

September 2022 5 cases

Featured: Yogi · Kevin · Pillya · Maggie · Dabbu

September brought triumphant homecomings alongside new emergencies. Yogi came home free of both his tick fever and the kilogram-heavy growth he had carried on his head — healed and back to his territory. Kevin, the torn-jaw pup, found his forever home, a joyful end to his hard rescue. Pillya returned to his village after more than a month, prancing happily on three legs, a cheerful tripod. But the streets kept giving: Maggie, an abandoned Pitbull mix, was picked up with a huge maggot wound in her ear, and Dabbu was diagnosed with parvovirus and admitted for intensive treatment — an ominous foreshadowing of the outbreak to come among Hope's pups.

October 2022 4 cases

Featured: Whitey · Jabya · Parvo pups · Rani

October was one of the Foundation's darkest months, marked by the fallout of a botched sterilisation at a popular shelter. Whitey came back from that shelter paralysed — no one could explain how — and spent the month in foster undergoing laser, physiotherapy and acupuncture. Jabya, neutered in the same batch, was found to have had his surgery go terribly wrong. The parvo puppies — Hope/Rani's litter — suffered devastating losses, three of seven gone and the rest fighting for their lives. And Rani (Hope) herself, still searching for her hospitalised babies, was hit by a bike and left with deep leg injuries. It was a month of compounding grief.

November 2022 4 cases

Featured: Whitey · Jabya · Parvo survivors · Rani's pups

November continued the struggle. Of Hope/Rani's nine puppies, five ultimately pulled through the gastro/parvo outbreak — a bittersweet outcome, mourning those lost while celebrating the survivors, who remained in and out of the shelter. Jabya's case grew clearer and more distressing: the surgeon had damaged or stitched his urethra during the botched neuter, leaving him unable to pass urine normally. And in an important turn, Whitey was found to have been misdiagnosed — she was not paralysed at all, but had a Spirocerca lump; a wasted month of physio and laser had worsened her condition, redirecting her toward the surgery she truly needed.

December 2022 2 cases

Featured: Whitey · Rumi

December centred on Whitey's hard-won recovery. Whitey underwent amputation surgery early in the month and, twelve hours later, showed remarkable strength and grace in accepting her new reality; within a fortnight she was walking and running happily, her vets touchingly writing 'Get well soon Whitey' on her amputation site, and she took to sitting among the greenery at sunset. Rumi, a loving young dog abandoned by his family simply because he had fallen ill, was rescued, treated with injections procured just in time, and returned healed and happy — a reminder that, alongside human cruelty, timely help can still rewrite an animal's fate.

January 2023 4 cases

Featured: Bruno · Whitey · Snowy/Ginger/Maggie · Awareness case

The new year opened with urgent appeals and a homecoming. Bruno, healthy until a day earlier, was found in a critical state and needed emergency funds; the Foundation cleared his initial bills with donors' help while he remained not out of danger. A harrowing awareness case came to light — a dog whose four legs were crushed when a bus ran over her as she slept — shared to press for compassion on the roads. Snowy, Ginger and Maggie, the pups whose spay surgeries had been botched, spent the new year back at the shelter with altered medication. And Whitey finally came home after five long months — a happy tripod, back where she belongs.

February 2023 3 cases

Featured: Patchy · Gundu Ma · Rano

February brought new admissions and enduring old friends. Patchy (also called Kalu or Bangaru), a young pup the founder had known for months, joined the Karmanye clan and settled into foster care, eating well and taking his medication on time. Gundu Ma, the Foundation's 'Oldie Goldie' of nearly sixteen — one of the very first dogs the founder ever fed, still cared for years after moving from her area — was looked after with special tenderness. And Rano, reported after a car hit, was watched closely when a painkiller did not resolve her limp, prompting further examination.

March 2023 4 cases

Featured: Pillu · Bholi · Chotu

The year closed with a demanding March. Pillu, a gentle boy from a construction site, collapsed suddenly and, after three vets, was diagnosed with tick-bite paralysis — unable to move his limbs but retaining deep pain sensation, which gave the vets hope; his case would carry into the next year. Bholi, a timid senior of around twelve whom the founder had fed and cared for over a decade, was found to have cancer that had spread and failing kidneys, and though not in evident pain, was moved onto a gentler line of treatment. Chotu, known for over seven years, was admitted after persistent vomiting that on-site care could not settle. The founder's candid weekend note — sitting alone, listing the rescues she was single-handedly juggling and their unfunded bills — captured the year's relentless load.

This report is a true summary of the charitable activities carried out by Karmanye Foundation during the financial year 1 April 2022 to 31 March 2023, based on the Foundation's field and publication records.
