During the financial year 2021–22, Karmanye Foundation continued its core charitable mission of relieving the suffering of community and street animals in and around Pune. The Foundation operates on a report-driven model: cases are reported by citizens, feeders, guards and volunteers; the animal is picked up and assessed; veterinary treatment or surgery is arranged at private clinics or paid shelters; and, wherever possible, the animal is rehabilitated and released back to its territory or placed in a permanent home. Alongside individual rescues, the Foundation undertook sterilisation and vaccination drives, community feeding and water-bowl provision during summer, senior and palliative care, support for elderly human feeders, and public advocacy for animal welfare and justice.

This report summarises the Foundation's activities month by month across the year. Approximately 33 representative cases have been documented from the Foundation's field records, selected to reflect the range and scale of work undertaken. FY 2021-22 was among the Foundation's earliest, founding-era years (the Section 8 company was incorporated in 2021), so the caseload and posting volume were lower than in later years. Each monthly entry is a narrative account of the principal cases and initiatives handled during that month.

Section 3

Month-by-Month Activity Record

April 2021 2 cases

Featured: Sania · Gundi

The year opened with two cases close to home. Sania, a dog the founder looks after in her own society, cut herself badly and was rushed for treatment; her recovery was complicated when a nerve appeared to be pinched and her uninjured paw developed a separate problem, and she repeatedly tore off her bandage and e-collar, re-injuring her healing foot. Gundi, an old girl being treated at a boarding facility for a stubborn skin condition, showed real improvement — no longer itching — and settled in for a further couple of weeks of care before she could go home. These early cases set the pattern of the Foundation's work: hands-on, persistent and centred on the animals of the founder's own feeding rounds.

May 2021 2 cases

Featured: Sania · Gundi

May brought two homecomings. Sania underwent amputation surgery at a partner facility and, a few days later, was brought home to recover; healed and walking on three legs, she returned to her society to the watchful care of the people who love her. Gundi too was back in her ilaka (territory), largely healed and free of the itching that had plagued her, thrilled to rejoin her gang. For a young organisation, these were affirming outcomes — difficult cases seen through from crisis to a safe return home.

June 2021 3 cases

Featured: Moti · Foxy & Alexa (sterilisation) · Children's cards

June combined rescue, sterilisation and a heartening moment of recognition. Moti, an aggressive and hard-to-catch dog, was reported with a large tumour on his leg; after finally being caught he was admitted for treatment, his wound slowly healing. The Foundation sent two female dogs, Foxy and Alexa, for sterilisation through ResQ Charitable Trust's Sahjeevan programme — part of its commitment to humane population control. And in a touching aside, the founder learned that the Foundation's work had inspired a story in a children's book by author Anita Satyajit, with children sending in thank-you cards — an early sign of the awareness the work was spreading.

July 2021 3 cases

Featured: Zoey · Amber · Ruku

July was a busy month of orthopaedic rescues. Zoey, who had been found paralysed and dragging herself after a hit-and-run and treated over some three months, returned to her territory in the labour colony where she lives. Amber, a gentle, affectionate dog with striking amber eyes, was reported injured and taken up for a fracture requiring a plate; put in a cast, she awaited surgery, cheered on by many well-wishers. Ruku, one of the pups the founder feeds daily, was found limping and diagnosed with a fracture; immobilised in a smaller cage and re-plastered after her wound was dressed, she began a long recovery.

August 2021 3 cases

Featured: Amber · Ruku · Pandya

August brought recoveries and a fresh rescue. Amber healed steadily after her fracture — her follow-up x-rays clear — and by month's end was released back to her territory, excited to be on familiar ground and quick to reassert herself among old friends and rivals. Ruku's x-rays brought great news: her fracture had begun to fuse, sparing her surgery. And Pandya, one of the Foundation's gentle regulars often bullied by tougher dogs in the galli, was taken up for care — another quiet rescue in a month of steady progress.

September 2021 5 cases

Featured: Buddhu · Ruku · Zoey · Hit-and-run pup · Kali

September was one of the busiest months of the Foundation's early period. Ruku, her fracture healed after nearly two months in foster, was spayed and released. Zoey, recovered from her earlier paralysis, was cleared for release in her area. Several new cases arrived at once: Buddhu, a very old dog the founder feeds, was found with a huge maggot wound on his paw, bleeding and infested, and was treated and admitted; and a hit-and-run puppy, paralysed and callously dropped off in a feeding area by whoever hit him, was admitted for care. The Foundation also funded a paid spay surgery for Kali, choosing prevention over the far harder task of finding homes for unwanted litters.

October 2021 3 cases

Featured: Buddu · Gundi · Chriscross collaboration

October's focus was on senior dogs and a welcome collaboration. Buddu, recovered from his maggot wound, developed an eye injury and grew increasingly frail, mostly sitting in one place; supporters rallied to buy him gravy pouches to tempt his appetite. Gundi was readmitted for her recurring skin condition, which had her scratching uncontrollably despite her age. The Foundation also entered a heartening collaboration with the Chriscross shop and The Green Bonneville, who chose to donate the profits of a specially designed merchandise line — an early boost to the organisation's fundraising and visibility.

November 2021 3 cases

Featured: Maple · Buddu · Diwali helpers

November held both a devastating loss and an urgent new rescue. After a long, tender decline, the Foundation's beloved senior Buddu passed away, mourned in a heartfelt farewell — a reminder that dogs often hide when their bodies begin to fail. Amid that grief came Maple, a five-month-old puppy hit in a hit-and-run (the second such case within an hour, the other killed on the spot) who suffered three fractures — two on her leg and one on her hip; she underwent surgery to insert a plate in her hip joint and to align and plaster the other breaks. The founder also used Diwali to thank the tapri workers, guards and helpers who make the work possible on the ground.

December 2021 3 cases

Featured: Maple · Kalu · Zoey

December brought recovery, strain and another loss. Maple continued recovering from her surgery at the shelter, needing extended rest before she could walk properly again. Kalu, a dog the founder had fed for years and had been treating for TVT (a transmissible venereal tumour), lost his battle and crossed the rainbow bridge, mourned in a moving tribute. Zoey remained an ongoing concern amid a rush of cases; the founder wrote candidly of reaching a breaking point with so many rescues, so little manpower and such limited funds, yet holding on for the animals' sake.

January 2022 2 cases

Featured: Zoey · Gratitude

January was quiet but reflective. Zoey was returned to her territory, apparently well and happy to be back with her friends, with the Foundation resolving to keep a close watch on her. Confined at home and unwell for a few days, the founder wrote a note of gratitude for the network of supporters who kept the doggies cared for even when she could not be on the ground — a glimpse of the community that had grown around the Foundation's work.

February 2022 1 case

Featured: Moti

February was a single-case month centred on Moti, who was recovering well at a boarding facility, winning over everyone there with his loving nature. His wound was dressed twice daily and he remained on medication, with the vet consulted again to chart the next steps in his treatment — steady, patient care of the kind that defines the Foundation's approach even in its quietest months.

March 2022 3 cases

Featured: Kabira · Moti · Tiger

The year closed with three cases. Kabira, an overweight, good-tempered dog found abandoned or lost outside the society with full-blown mange, was picked up and admitted, his coat visibly recovering and weight reducing over the weeks. Moti's long treatment culminated in a tail amputation surgery, which went well — though the spirited boy promptly broke three e-collars and opened some sutures. And Tiger, the beloved dog of a labourer, Mr Kamble, in a Kharadi labour camp, was treated and sent home to a family who — despite living in a tiny space — held boundless love for him, closing the Foundation's earliest documented year on a warm note.

This report is a true summary of the charitable activities carried out by Karmanye Foundation during the financial year 1 April 2021 to 31 March 2022, based on the Foundation's field and publication records.
