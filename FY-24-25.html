During the financial year 2024–25, Karmanye Foundation continued its core charitable mission of relieving the suffering of community and street animals in and around Pune. The Foundation operates on a report-driven model: cases are reported by citizens, feeders, guards and volunteers; the animal is picked up and assessed; veterinary treatment or surgery is arranged at private clinics or paid shelters; and, wherever possible, the animal is rehabilitated and released back to its territory or placed in a permanent home. Alongside individual rescues, the Foundation undertook sterilisation and vaccination drives, community feeding and water-bowl provision during summer, senior and palliative care, support for elderly human feeders, and public advocacy for animal welfare and justice.

This report summarises the Foundation's activities month by month across the year. Approximately 54 representative cases have been documented from the Foundation's field records, selected to reflect the range and scale of work undertaken. Each monthly entry is a narrative account of the principal cases and initiatives handled during that month.

Section 3

Month-by-Month Activity Record

April 2024 2 cases

Featured: Sweety · Julie

April opened with two ongoing cases. Sweety underwent surgery to remove three tumours and began recovery at a foster; the spirited girl repeatedly worked off her E-collar and reopened her stitches, turning a routine recovery into a longer course of open-wound care, with the Foundation meeting surgery and blood-work costs of around ₹26,300. Mid-month brought a happy homecoming: Julie, a locality favourite, returned after a long forty-day stay at a partner shelter where she had been correctly diagnosed, treated and much loved, before coming back to the neighbourhood that had missed her.

May 2024 3 cases

Featured: Pillya · Julie (monsoon shelter) · Sweety

May was defined by the start of a hard fight. Pillya, a young, much-loved dog belonging to a security guard's family, was reported with swollen hind legs and a wobbly gait and, after tests, diagnosed with low-grade lymphoma. Given his youth, the Foundation chose to give him a fighting chance and began a nineteen-week chemotherapy protocol at a partner clinic, completing his first and second (oral) rounds during the month with a volunteer transporting him to and fro. Sweety continued her slow recovery from her reopened surgical wounds. The Foundation also built Julie a temporary monsoon shelter, funded by well-wishers, and ran a small fundraising appeal around Akshaya Tritiya.

June 2024 3 cases

Featured: Sweety · Yuki · Pillya

June brought both a milestone and a growing worry. Sweety, healed at last after more than a month at the shelter, was released happy and tumour-free; her full treatment — surgery, stay, medication and transport — came to nearly ₹60,000. The Foundation ran an adoption drive for Yuki, a healthy, affectionate young boy for whom a shelter could only ever be a temporary haven, appealing for a loving home or farm. Pillya, meanwhile, pressed on through his third and fourth chemotherapy cycles; after the heavy fourth round he grew very low — not eating, with loose motions — and was sent for a cardiac check, as the drug is hard on the heart.

July 2024 5 cases

Featured: Pillya · Kalu/Bhalu · Radha · Julie

July was a month of resilience and heartbreak. Pillya reached his seventh and eighth chemotherapy cycles, responding well, with treatment and transport costs mounting past ₹57,000 — but later in the month the brave boy passed away, a profound loss for the Foundation and for his elderly rescuer, the construction-site guard who had raised him from twelve days old. Kalu/Bhalu, one of the Foundation's oldest dogs, underwent surgery to remove a large abdominal tumour and another festering in his mouth that had made eating difficult, and began recovery at a private shelter. Radha, a senior of around ten years fed outside the society, was taken up for a deep gash. A detailed adoption appeal was also shared for Julie.

August 2024 5 cases

Featured: Mimi's pups · Debbie · Radha · Kalu/Bhalu

August was marked by maternal tragedy and difficult healing. Mimi, a mother dog reported in severe distress while unable to deliver, could not be saved, but her six orphaned puppies were taken in and, after an anxious search, placed with a foster and a surrogate mother, Coco, to be hand-reared — a long journey the Foundation committed to fund. Radha reopened her stitches despite careful nursing, so her wounds had to be managed as open wounds with repeated vet visits. Kalu/Bhalu healed from his tumour surgery but developed a worrying swelling under the chin, likely a lymph node, which the team monitored closely. Debbie, a friendly five-to-six-month-old Doberman abandoned and living off a garbage bin in Kharadi, was rescued.

September 2024 5 cases

Featured: Kaya · Moti · Snow · Mimi's pups

September brought a swift rescue-to-home story alongside continuing care. Kaya, a puppy whose jaw was torn and teeth broken in a hit-and-run on the eve of Ganesh Chaturthi, was sutured and moved to a paid shelter on soft food; within weeks, in an unexpected 'Sunday miracle', she found a permanent home. Moti, the beloved boy from the petrol pump who had earlier lost his tail to infection, saw the infection return and was taken back into care. The Foundation continued raising funds for Snow and for Mimi's puppies, whose eyes were opening to the world under Coco's patient mothering.

October 2024 5 cases

Featured: Moti · Snow · Radha · Debbie/Onyx · Baba

October combined long recoveries with heartening support. Moti's stubborn wound, resistant to healing for over twenty days, was cleaned and treated under anaesthesia at a clinic and slowly improved at Yeshu's shelter. Snow, the old man, healed beautifully and — remarkably — a donor stepped forward to take lifelong responsibility for him. Radha returned to her area after three months of healing that had included spay and mammary-gland surgery. Debbie, now renamed Onyx, was placed with a breed-adoption foundation while she awaited a forever home. And for the senior dog Baba, six donors pledged ₹1,000 monthly for life, enabling his move to an animal home for elderly dogs.

November 2024 6 cases

Featured: Ledgu · Vishnu · Bhura · Teddy · Suzie · Baba

November was, in the Foundation's own words, an overwhelming month of rescues. Ledgu, wrongly picked up and held for four days by another party with no treatment, was recovered and taken into proper care. Vishnu, a young dog from the feeding gang, was admitted for a deep gash that on-site medication could not heal. Two seniors were taken up out of compassion for their age — Bhura, reported by kind guards, unable to walk well or eat, and another frail old 'baba' found at a society gate. Teddy, a dog the Foundation had known since puppyhood, was brought in. And tiny Suzie, barely two months old, was driven down from Panchgani to a Pune clinic, with a foster arranged for her onward care.

December 2024 6 cases

Featured: Ledgu · Bhura · Teddy · Suzie · Baba · Lalya

December was a month of releases and one worsening case. Ledgu completed a full month of treatment and was released, happily back on his ledge. Bhura, healed after treatment costing around ₹22,000, was released to his location with the site guards willing to continue his nerve medication. Teddy showed a renewed willingness to walk and was started on laser therapy. Suzie, feisty and much improved, was dewormed, vaccinated and treated for mange at her Panchgani foster. Baba was brought in for a swollen paw and prescribed supplements for the hard winter. The month's hardest case was Lalya (Golu), who suffered repeated major seizures and declined despite medication, unable to stand and eating only with help.

January 2025 5 cases

Featured: Sweety · Nora · Bablu · Imli · Bhola (Benny)

The new year began, tragically, at 2 a.m. on 1 January when Sweety, one of the Foundation's old girls, was hit by a vehicle and left crying in pain. Days later Nora was run over by a resident's vehicle whose driver simply went home to sleep; she was caught and rushed to the vet. Bablu, a happy, gentle boy, was struck in a hit-and-run and left in a fragile, critical state requiring an emergency facility. Imli was reported with a grave eye injury and admitted for treatment. And Benny (Bhola), one of the oldest dogs the Foundation feeds, collapsed and was taken up for age-related decline. It was a punishing start to the year, dominated by road-accident emergencies.

February 2025 4 cases

Featured: Browny · Bablu · Imli

February centred on two fighters recovering from road accidents. Browny, hit in a hit-and-run and left with a completely swollen, hanging hind leg and multiple fractures, was driven to the shelter by a good Samaritan; through a combination of Ayurveda, supplements, allopathy and steady care he defied the odds and was soon using all four legs, needing a few more weeks to strengthen the bones. Bablu, critical in January, improved remarkably — walking on his own and regaining normal function — thanks to being admitted to a hospital rather than a crowded shelter. Both Browny and Imli continued healing at the K9 shelter, where their wounds were kept clean and dressed until they were fit for release.

March 2025 5 cases

Featured: Veer · Poppy · Rani · Pillu · Browny

The year closed with dramatic rescues and steady recoveries. Veer was pulled to safety after being trapped in a drainage for days, freed only when the PMC opened it and a volunteer climbed down to bring him up. Poppy, reported with cloudy eyes, was found to have an infection and temporary blindness and was started on a full course of medication. Rani was rushed in after back-to-back seizures, caught in time by an alert guard and feeder. Pillu continued healing from pelvic injuries, taking well to a supportive brace, though his stay was extended when the vet found pain in his other hind leg. And Browny — the February fighter — was released back to his location, strong and happy to be home.

This report is a true summary of the charitable activities carried out by Karmanye Foundation during the financial year 1 April 2024 to 31 March 2025, based on the Foundation's field and publication records.
